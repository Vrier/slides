Week 12 · Readings

Modals & Tense

Reading list Figures: Condoravdi · Hacquard · Bhatt

Four sources for Week 12 — two textbook surveys that close on the tense / aspect / modality interactions, and two contemporary papers (the actuality-entailment argument and a current cross-linguistic theory). References are in APA; open-access links are given where available. Read the surveys first, then Hacquard. Confirm editions and availability against the library / Blackboard list before circulating.

Reading list two textbook · two contemporary
  1. TextbookHacquard, V. (2011). Modality. In K. von Heusinger, C. Maienborn, & P. Portner (Eds.), Semantics: An international handbook of natural language meaning (Vol. 2, pp. 1484–1515). de Gruyter Mouton.
    The survey that closes on exactly this week's question — how modal interpretation depends on tense and aspect.
    Library login DOI
  2. TextbookPortner, P. (2009). Modality and other categories. In Modality (chap. 5). Oxford University Press.
    The textbook's concluding chapter on how modality relates to tense, aspect, and mood — the frame for the week.
    Purchase
  3. ContemporaryHacquard, V. (2009). On the interaction of aspect and modal auxiliaries. Linguistics and Philosophy, 32(3), 279–315. https://doi.org/10.1007/s10988-009-9061-6
    The actuality-entailment puzzle — why a root modal under perfective aspect entails that its complement actually happened.
    Library login DOI
  4. ContemporaryRullmann, H., & Matthewson, L. (2018). Towards a theory of modal-temporal interaction. Language, 94(2), 281–331. https://doi.org/10.1353/lan.2018.0018
    A current cross-linguistic synthesis — how a single theory can predict the temporal orientation of modals across languages.
    Library login DOI
