Week 2 · Readings

Negation

Reading list Figures: Klima · Ladusaw · Horn

Three sources for Week 2 — a textbook survey of polarity, a classical reference on the landscape of negation, and a contemporary paper refining the licensing condition. References are in APA; open-access links are given where available. Read Giannakidou first; von Fintel rewards close reading after the lecture on downward entailment. Confirm editions and availability against the library / Blackboard list before circulating.

Reading list one textbook · one classical · one contemporary
  1. TextbookGiannakidou, A. (2011). Positive and negative polarity items. In K. von Heusinger, C. Maienborn, & P. Portner (Eds.), Semantics: An international handbook of natural language meaning (Vol. 2, pp. 1660–1712). de Gruyter Mouton.
    The standard survey — downward entailment, NPI / PPI licensing, and negative concord across languages.
    Purchase
  2. ClassicalHorn, L. R. (1989). A natural history of negation. University of Chicago Press. (Reissued 2001, CSLI Publications)
    The magisterial reference on negation — logical, linguistic, and philosophical. Dip in by topic rather than reading whole.
    Purchase
  3. Contemporaryvon Fintel, K. (1999). NPI licensing, Strawson entailment, and context dependency. Journal of Semantics, 16(2), 97–148. https://doi.org/10.1093/jos/16.2.97
    Strawson entailment as the NPI licenser — how presuppositions and context dependency rescue the Fauconnier–Ladusaw analysis.
    Open access Open PDF
