Week 11 · Readings

Evidentiality

Reading list Figures: Aikhenvald · Faller · Matthewson

Six sources for Week 11 — three textbook treatments (an epistemic-modal framing and two typological surveys), two classical sources for the illocutionary and modal analyses, and a contemporary fieldwork-based modal account. References are in APA; open-access links are given where available. Read Aikhenvald first, then Faller. Confirm editions and availability against the library / Blackboard list before circulating.

Reading list three textbook · two classical · one contemporary
  1. TextbookPortner, P. (2009). Modality and evidentiality. In Modality (chap. 4, §4.3). Oxford University Press.
    Evidentials weighed against epistemic modals — the framing question that organises the week.
    Purchase
  2. TextbookAikhenvald, A. Y. (2004). Evidentiality in grammar; Evidentiality and other categories. In Evidentiality (chaps. 1–2). Oxford University Press.
    The foundational typological survey — what an evidential system is, and the shapes they take across languages.
    Purchase
  3. TextbookAikhenvald, A. Y. (2018). Evidentiality: The framework. In A. Y. Aikhenvald (Ed.), The Oxford handbook of evidentiality (pp. 1–43). Oxford University Press.
    The updated survey — a current map of evidential categories and the diagnostics for them.
    Library login DOI
  4. ClassicalFaller, M. T. (2002). Semantics and pragmatics of evidentials in Cuzco Quechua (chaps. 1 & 3) [Doctoral dissertation, Stanford University].
    The illocutionary analysis — evidentials as modifiers of the speech act, not the proposition. The keystone of the debate.
    Open access Open PDF
  5. ClassicalIzvorski, R. (1997). The present perfect as an epistemic modal. In A. Lawson (Ed.), Proceedings of SALT 7 (pp. 222–239). CLC Publications.
    The other camp — indirect evidentiality analysed as epistemic modality, founding the modal approach.
    Open access Open PDF
  6. ContemporaryMatthewson, L., Davis, H., & Rullmann, H. (2007). Evidentials as epistemic modals: Evidence from St'át'imcets. Linguistic Variation Yearbook, 7(1), 201–254. https://doi.org/10.1075/livy.7.07mat
    The modal analysis backed by detailed fieldwork — evidentials as modals with an evidential presupposition.
    Library login DOI
