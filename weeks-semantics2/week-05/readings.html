Week 5 · Readings

Attitude Reports

Reading list Figures: Frege · Hintikka · Quine

Four sources for Week 5 — two textbook treatments of propositional attitudes, the classical possible-worlds analysis of belief and knowledge, and a contemporary paper on how attitudes interact with embedded epistemics. References are in APA; open-access links are given where available. Read a textbook chapter first, then Hintikka. Confirm editions and availability against the library / Blackboard list before circulating.

Reading list two textbook · one classical · one contemporary
  1. Textbookvon Fintel, K., & Heim, I. (2011). Propositional attitudes. In Intensional semantics (chap. 2) [Lecture notes]. Massachusetts Institute of Technology.
    The Hintikka semantics built compositionally — believe and know as quantifiers over doxastic alternatives.
    Open access Open PDF
  2. TextbookCoppock, E., & Champollion, L. (2024). Attitude verbs. In Invitation to formal semantics (chap. 13, §13.3) [Unpublished manuscript]. Boston University & New York University.
    A lighter route into the same idea — attitudes as quantification over accessible worlds, worked step by step.
    Open access Open PDF
  3. ClassicalHintikka, J. (1969). Semantics for propositional attitudes. In J. W. Davis, D. J. Hockney, & W. K. Wilson (Eds.), Philosophical logic (pp. 21–45). D. Reidel.
    The founding analysis — belief and knowledge as quantification over epistemically accessible worlds.
    Purchase
  4. ContemporaryAnand, P., & Hacquard, V. (2013). Epistemics and attitudes. Semantics and Pragmatics, 6(8), 1–59. https://doi.org/10.3765/sp.6.8
    How attitude verbs license embedded epistemic modals — a cross-linguistic window on the structure of attitudes.
    Open access Open PDF
