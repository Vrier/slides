Week 8 · Readings

Indicatives & the Restrictor Analysis

Reading list Figures: Kratzer · Lewis · Stalnaker

Four sources for Week 8 — a textbook presentation of the restrictor analysis, two classical sources (the selection-function semantics and the triviality result that bears on it), and a contemporary survey. References are in APA; open-access links are given where available. Read von Fintel & Heim first, then Stalnaker. Confirm editions and availability against the library / Blackboard list before circulating.

Reading list one textbook · two classical · one contemporary
  1. Textbookvon Fintel, K., & Heim, I. (2011). Conditionals as restrictors. In Intensional semantics [Lecture notes]. Massachusetts Institute of Technology.
    The restrictor analysis stated compositionally — if-clauses restrict an operator rather than forming a two-place connective.
    Open access Open PDF
  2. ClassicalStalnaker, R. C. (1968). A theory of conditionals. In N. Rescher (Ed.), Studies in logical theory (pp. 98–112). Blackwell.
    The selection-function semantics — the nearest-world account, and the main rival to the restrictor view.
    Library login DOI
  3. ClassicalLewis, D. (1976). Probabilities of conditionals and conditional probabilities. The Philosophical Review, 85(3), 297–315. https://doi.org/10.2307/2184045
    The triviality result — why the probability of a conditional cannot in general be the conditional probability.
    Library login JSTOR
  4. ContemporaryEdgington, D. (2025). Indicative conditionals. In E. N. Zalta & U. Nodelman (Eds.), The Stanford Encyclopedia of Philosophy (Summer 2025 ed.). Metaphysics Research Lab, Stanford University.
    The state of the debate — material, suppositional, and possible-worlds accounts weighed against each other.
    Open access SEP
