Week 4 · Readings

The Kratzer System

Reading list Figures: Kratzer

Four sources for Week 4 — two textbook treatments of the conversational background, and two classical Kratzer papers (the early foundational analysis, and the paper that introduces the modal base and ordering source). References are in APA; open-access links are given where available. Read a textbook chapter first, then Kratzer 1977, then 1981. Confirm editions and availability against the library / Blackboard list before circulating.

Reading list two textbook · two classical
  1. TextbookPortner, P. (2009). The standard theory: Modal force and conversational backgrounds. In Modality (chaps. 3–4). Oxford University Press.
    The fullest textbook account of modal base, ordering source, and graded modality — the spine of this week.
    Purchase
  2. Textbookvon Fintel, K., & Heim, I. (2011). Modality (doubly-relative modality). In Intensional semantics (chap. 3) [Lecture notes]. Massachusetts Institute of Technology.
    The Kratzerian system reconstructed compositionally — how the two conversational backgrounds combine in the denotation.
    Open access Open PDF
  3. ClassicalKratzer, A. (1977). What "must" and "can" must and can mean. Linguistics and Philosophy, 1(3), 337–355. https://doi.org/10.1007/BF00353453
    The earliest foundational paper — modal force plus a contextually supplied conversational background.
    Library login DOI
  4. ClassicalKratzer, A. (1981). The notional category of modality. In H.-J. Eikmeyer & H. Rieser (Eds.), Words, worlds, and contexts: New approaches in word semantics (pp. 38–74). de Gruyter.
    The mature system — modal base and ordering source as two conversational backgrounds, and doubly-relative modality.
    Purchase
