Week 3 · Readings

Worlds, Intensions & Modal Logic

Reading list Figures: Kripke · von Wright

Five sources for Week 3 — three textbook treatments that build the possible-worlds machinery (◻/◇, accessibility, K/T/S4/S5), a classical source for accessibility semantics, and a contemporary survey of the modal-logic systems. References are in APA; open-access links are given where available. Read a textbook chapter first, then Kripke. Confirm editions and availability against the library / Blackboard list before circulating.

Reading list three textbook · one classical · one contemporary
  1. Textbookvon Fintel, K., & Heim, I. (2011). Modality. In Intensional semantics (chap. 3) [Lecture notes]. Massachusetts Institute of Technology.
    Modal operators and quantification over accessible worlds, inside the compositional framework — continuous with Week 1.
    Open access Open PDF
  2. TextbookCoppock, E., & Champollion, L. (2024). Modality and intensionality. In Invitation to formal semantics (chap. 13, §§13.1–13.2, 13.4) [Unpublished manuscript]. Boston University & New York University.
    Necessity and possibility, intension vs. extension, and how worlds are represented explicitly in the model.
    Open access Open PDF
  3. TextbookPortner, P. (2009). Modal logic; The standard theory of modality. In Modality (chaps. 1–2). Oxford University Press.
    The deepest of the three on the possible-worlds and modal-logic background — read for the systems and their frame conditions.
    Purchase
  4. ClassicalKripke, S. A. (1963). Semantical considerations on modal logic. Acta Philosophica Fennica, 16, 83–94.
    Accessibility-relation semantics — the move that makes ◻ and ◇ quantifiers over worlds, and the keystone for the K/T/S4/S5 hierarchy.
    Purchase
  5. ContemporaryGarson, J. (2024). Modal logic. In E. N. Zalta & U. Nodelman (Eds.), The Stanford Encyclopedia of Philosophy (Spring 2024 ed.). Metaphysics Research Lab, Stanford University.
    A clean modern statement of frames and the K/T/S4/S5 systems — mirrors the formal machinery of the lecture.
    Open access SEP
