Common nouns and VPs denote sets. Write \(A\) for the restrictor, \(B\) for the nuclear scope, and \(\mathrm{DET}(A)(B)\) for a determiner's truth condition. Use only \(\subseteq,\cap,\setminus,\varnothing,|\cdot|\). 4ConservativityFormal6 marks

A determiner is conservative iff \(\mathrm{DET}(A)(B)\Leftrightarrow\mathrm{DET}(A)(A\cap B)\).

(a)For each, state whether it is conservative (justify briefly): \(\mathbf{some}\), \(\mathbf{no}\), \(\mathbf{most}\), \(\mathbf{at\ least\ two}\), \(\mathbf{ex2}\), \(\mathbf{equi}\), \(\mathbf{even}\), \(\mathbf{b35}\).
(b)Find one of the eight (if any) that is not conservative and give a model proving it.
5Monotonicity in \(B\)Formal5 marks

Upward: \(B\subseteq C\Rightarrow[\mathrm{DET}(A)(B)\Rightarrow\mathrm{DET}(A)(C)]\). Downward: \(B\subseteq C\Rightarrow[\mathrm{DET}(A)(C)\Rightarrow\mathrm{DET}(A)(B)]\).

(a)Classify each as upward, downward, or neither in \(B\): \(\mathbf{some}\), \(\mathbf{no}\), \(\mathbf{most}\), \(\mathbf{at\ least\ two}\), \(\mathbf{ex2}\), \(\mathbf{equi}\), \(\mathbf{even}\), \(\mathbf{b35}\).
(b)Using mammal ⊆ animal, show every is upward but not downward monotone.
6Universals & polarityTypological4 marks
(a)Conservativity is offered as a semantic universal. State it in one sentence and explain why it is surprising that languages draw only from the conservative determiners.
(b)Downward-monotone determiners license NPIs (no student read any books vs *every student read any books). Which of your Q5 answers predict NPI licensing? Test one in a language you know.
