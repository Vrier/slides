Week 5 · Readings

Quantifiers and Scope

Reading list Figures: Frege · Russell

Four sources for Week 5 — two textbook chapters (one for predicate-logic quantification, one for scope), a classical paper on the quantificational analysis of denoting phrases, and a contemporary survey of scope mechanisms. References are in APA; open-access links are given where available. Read the two textbook chapters first; Russell rewards a second pass. Confirm editions and availability against the library / Blackboard list before circulating.

Reading list two textbook · one classical · one contemporary
  1. TextbookCoppock, E., & Champollion, L. (2024). Predicate logic. In Invitation to formal semantics (chap. 3, 2nd part) [Unpublished manuscript]. Boston University & New York University.
    Quantifiers ∀ and ∃, variables, and binding — the predicate-logic machinery this week introduces.
    Open access Open PDF
  2. TextbookKearns, K. (2011). Scope and scopal ambiguity. In Semantics (2nd ed., chap. 3, §3.5). Palgrave Macmillan.
    Scope and scopal ambiguity worked through tree diagrams — the bridge from notation to interpretation.
    Purchase
  3. ClassicalRussell, B. (1905). On denoting. Mind, 14(56), 479–493. https://doi.org/10.1093/mind/XIV.4.479
    Denoting phrases as quantifiers, and the original scope distinction — primary vs secondary occurrence (the King of France).
    Library login JSTOR
  4. ContemporaryRuys, E. G., & Winter, Y. (2011). Quantifier scope in formal linguistics. In D. M. Gabbay & F. Guenthner (Eds.), Handbook of philosophical logic (2nd ed., Vol. 16, pp. 159–225). Springer.
    The standard modern survey of scope mechanisms — inverse scope, scope islands, and how theories handle them.
    Open access Open PDF
