Empirical Why do adverbs entail the unmodified sentence?
Data

Jones buttered the toast slowly, in the kitchen, with a knife ⊨ Jones buttered the toast. Any subset of adjuncts may be dropped.

Argument vs adjunct

Arguments (subject, object) are selected by the verb, often obligatory, not freely repeatable. Adjuncts (adverbs, many PPs) are optional and stack freely. A good semantics keeps the contrast while predicting the entailments.

Reflect
1The bad idea:

Suppose adverbs were extra argument slots: \(\textsc{butter}(x_1,\dots,x_n)\). Explain why this is the wrong way to think about verbal meaning. Give three different adjunct types that would each demand a new slot.

Historical From Davidson's event argument to the neo-Davidsonian programme
Background

Davidson (1967) proposed action verbs carry a hidden event argument \(e\); adverbs are predicates of \(e\). Independent evidence that events are "things": event pronouns (Mary did it too), quantification (everything John did), and perception reports (John saw Mary buttering the toast). Parsons (1990) and others took the further neo-Davidsonian step of relating all participants to the event by thematic roles.

Formal Building the representation
Two representations

Davidsonian: \(\exists e[\textsc{butter}(j,t,e)\wedge\textsc{slow}(e)]\) — subject and object inside the verb. Neo-Davidsonian: \(\exists e[\textsc{butter}(e)\wedge\textsc{agent}(e,j)\wedge\textsc{theme}(e,t)\wedge\textsc{slow}(e)]\) — participants severed into roles.

Key idea

Each adjunct is a separate conjunct, so the entailment to a less-modified sentence is just conjunction elimination: \(\exists e[\textsc{butter}(j,t,e)\wedge\textsc{in}(e,k)\wedge\textsc{with}(e,n)]\models\exists e[\textsc{butter}(j,t,e)]\).

New tool
Davidsonian translation

Keep selected arguments inside the verb; existentially close the event; add each adjunct as a conjoined predicate of \(e\).

New tool
Why sever the arguments?

Neo-Davidsonian representations make alternations and causative/inchoative pairs share a core, with entailments by conjunct-dropping.

Apply it
1Give one neo-Davidsonian core for both load the truck with hay and load the hay onto the truck.
2Write neo-Davidsonian forms for Mary opened the door and The door opened, and show the entailment between them.
3The door was opened deliberately implies an agent. Give a representation that captures this; what does it cost the neo-Davidsonian?
Typological Where is event structure spelled out morphologically?
Overview

Case marking (instrumental, dative, ergative) overtly tags a participant's role; applicatives add a participant via a verbal affix (a morphological thematic-role conjunct); serial-verb constructions describe one complex event with several verbs sharing arguments — all visible support for an event variable and severed roles.

Cross-linguistic
1Your own data:

In a language you know, how is an instrument or a beneficiary marked (case, adposition, applicative)? Show how the marking corresponds to a neo-Davidsonian thematic-role conjunct.
