Week 8 · Readings

Verbs and Events

Reading list Figures: Davidson · Parsons

Three sources for Week 8 — a textbook chapter on event semantics, the classical paper that introduced the event argument, and a contemporary survey of the field. References are in APA; open-access links are given where available. Read the textbook chapter first, then Davidson. Confirm editions and availability against the library / Blackboard list before circulating.

Reading list one textbook · one classical · one contemporary
  1. TextbookCoppock, E., & Champollion, L. (2024). Event semantics. In Invitation to formal semantics (chap. 11, §§11.1, 11.3–11.6) [Unpublished manuscript]. Boston University & New York University.
    The neo-Davidsonian turn and composition with events. Skip §11.2 (Aktionsart) — that returns in Week 10.
    Open access Open PDF
  2. ClassicalDavidson, D. (1967). The logical form of action sentences. In N. Rescher (Ed.), The logic of decision and action (pp. 81–95). University of Pittsburgh Press.
    The founding argument — action verbs carry a hidden event argument (Jones buttered the toast slowly, in the bathroom, with a knife).
    Purchase
  3. ContemporaryMaienborn, C. (2011). Event semantics. In C. Maienborn, K. von Heusinger, & P. Portner (Eds.), Semantics: An international handbook of natural language meaning (Vol. 1, pp. 802–829). de Gruyter Mouton.
    The development from Davidson through the neo-Davidsonian approach to Kratzer's stage- / individual-level distinction.
    Purchase
