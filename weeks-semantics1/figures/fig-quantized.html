Cumulative vs quantized reference

Two properties of a denotation — and the mass≈plural parallel they expose

Krifka 1989 / 1998
“Nominal reference & temporal constitution”
Choose a predicate
?
Cumulative
\(\forall x,y\,[\,P(x)\wedge P(y)\rightarrow P(x\oplus y)\,]\)
Any sum of two P’s is again P.
?
Quantized
\(\forall x,y\,[\,P(x)\wedge P(y)\rightarrow \neg\,(y\sqsubset x)\,]\)
No proper part of a P is itself P.
Pick a predicate to test both properties. Mass nouns and bare plurals are cumulative; singular count and numeral phrases are quantized. The same split will reappear in the verb — as (a)telicity.
Cumulative reference ⇄ no atoms · quantized reference ⇄ bounded, atomic
