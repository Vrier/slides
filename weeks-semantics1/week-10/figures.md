## Fig 5 · Incremental theme — object→event homomorphism
**File:** `../figures/fig-incremental.html`  
**Interaction:** **▶ Play** auto-runs from 0→100%; the scrubber lets you drag to any point. The μ arrow follows the cut between eaten/uneaten; a gold star appears at culmination. Pause/resume.  
**Slot:** formal lens. The centrepiece figure for this week — makes the "measuring out" idea completely concrete. Use Play first, then drag the scrubber back to ~50% while explaining the homomorphism condition.

## Fig 6 · Telicity — where the finish line comes from
**File:** `../figures/fig-telicity.html`  
**Interaction:** toggle between **an apple** (quantized, telic) and **soup** (cumulative, atelic); press **▶ Run the event**. Telic: the bar fills to a culmination star. Atelic: the bar loops indefinitely with no endpoint. The *in*/*for* adverbial tests auto-update for each mode.  
**Slot:** formal lens. Good directly after Fig 5 — shows that the endpoint comes from the object, not the verb.

**TO DO / ideas:**
- Add a third option: *apples* (bare plural, cumulative) to show that bare-plural objects also produce atelic predicates.
- Could display the Verkuyl *BECOME* template updating live as the object type changes.
- A "stretch task" toggle to show how *for three hours, eat an apple* forces an iterative/completive reinterpretation.

## Fig 8 · Aktionsart — the Vendler classes
**File:** `../figures/fig-aktionsart.html`  
**Interaction:** click any cell in the 2×2 matrix (or the State tile below) to load its feature profile — ±Dynamic, ±Telic, ±Durative — and its three adverbial diagnostics (*is V-ing*, *for an hour*, *in an hour*) with ✓/✗ verdicts.  
**Slot:** formal lens. Use as a reference/summary figure at the close of the Aktionsart discussion; the click-through format makes it good for Q&A ("which class is this? let's test it").

**TO DO / ideas:**
