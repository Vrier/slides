Week 10 · Readings

Aktionsart and Time

Reading list Figures: Vendler · Bach

Four sources for Week 10 — two textbook chapters on aspectual classes, and two classical papers: the four-way classification of verbs, and the algebra that relates events to one another in time. References are in APA; open-access links are given where available. Read the textbook chapters first; Vendler is short and worth reading twice. Confirm editions and availability against the library / Blackboard list before circulating.

Reading list two textbook · two classical
  1. TextbookCoppock, E., & Champollion, L. (2024). Aktionsart. In Invitation to formal semantics (chap. 11, §11.2) [Unpublished manuscript]. Boston University & New York University.
    The aspectual classes within the event-semantic framework — the section we deferred from Week 8.
    Open access Open PDF
  2. TextbookKearns, K. (2011). Aktionsarten: Aspectual classes of events. In Semantics (2nd ed., chap. 8). Palgrave Macmillan.
    The Vendler classes with the diagnostic tests and graded exercises — the workhorse reading.
    Purchase
  3. ClassicalVendler, Z. (1957). Verbs and times. The Philosophical Review, 66(2), 143–160. https://doi.org/10.2307/2182371
    The original four-way classification — states, activities, accomplishments, achievements.
    Library login JSTOR
  4. ClassicalBach, E. (1986). The algebra of events. Linguistics and Philosophy, 9(1), 5–16. https://doi.org/10.1007/BF00627432
    Eventualities mirror the mass / count structure of nouns — telicity and temporal part-structure made algebraic. Ties Weeks 4 and 8 to time.
