Short written answers — two to four sentences each.

(a)Explain Frege's concept / object distinction. Why does treating giggles as an unsaturated function rather than a property of objects make a compositional theory possible?
(b)Tarski defined truth for languages with no ambiguity or context-dependence. Name one feature of natural language that resists direct application and sketch, in a sentence, how semanticists handle it.
6 Predication across languagesTypological 4 marks

English marks predication with an overt copula (is); Russian drops it in the present tense (Нил великан, 'Neil [is] a giant'); other languages use bare juxtaposition.

(a)Does the absence of a copula in Russian mean its predications have different truth conditions? Answer using the membership model (individual \(\in\) set).
(b)Choose a language you know. Show how Neil is tall is expressed, gloss it, and say whether the surface form changes the underlying semantic structure.
