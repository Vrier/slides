Predicates and Set Theory

Reading list Figures: Frege · Cantor

Three sources for Week 3 — a textbook chapter on the set-theoretic toolkit, a classical source for the predicate-as-function idea, and a contemporary reference for the formal apparatus. References are in APA; open-access links are given where available. Read the textbook chapter first; Frege rewards a second pass after the lecture. Confirm editions and availability against the library / Blackboard list before circulating.

Reading list one textbook · one classical · one contemporary
  1. TextbookCoppock, E., & Champollion, L. (2024). Sets and relations. In Invitation to formal semantics (chap. 2) [Unpublished manuscript]. Boston University & New York University.
    Membership, subsets, relations, and functions — predicates as characteristic functions of sets.
    Open access Open PDF
  2. ClassicalFrege, G. (1980). Function and concept (P. Geach, Trans.). In P. Geach & M. Black (Eds.), Translations from the philosophical writings of Gottlob Frege (3rd ed., pp. 21–41). Blackwell. (Original work published 1891)
    Concepts as functions from objects to truth-values — why a predicate is the characteristic function of a set.
    Purchase
  3. ContemporaryBagaria, J. (2020). Set theory. In E. N. Zalta (Ed.), The Stanford Encyclopedia of Philosophy (Spring 2020 ed.). Metaphysics Research Lab, Stanford University.
    The formal apparatus — membership, subsets, relations, functions. §§1–3 are the relevant part.
    Open access SEP
