## Topic: Attitude Verbs & Clausal Embedding
A proseminar on *what an attitude relates an agent to* — the through-line question that ties
the whole term together. Built in **two arcs**: Arc I assembles the formal tools, Arc II runs
them through the empirical phenomena.

## The two arcs

**Arc I — Formal Tools (wks 1–6).** Sense/reference/opacity (Frege, Russell, Carnap) → modal
frames & accessibility (Kripke) → attitudes as quantification, with Kratzer's conversational
backgrounds folded in (Hintikka, Kratzer) → de re/de dicto/**de se** and centred worlds (Lewis;
Percus & Sauerland) → the **granularity problem** and structured/impossible-worlds/attitudinal-object
responses (Cresswell & von Stechow; Moltmann) → the **hinge**: decomposing attitude verbs, clauses
as predicates of contentful eventualities (Kratzer 2006; Moulton; Davidson for contrast).

**Arc II — Empirical Phenomena (wk 8 + presentations).** The grammar of embedding run through
the puzzles. Only **Factivity** (wk 8) is taught; the remaining four phenomena are **parked** (below)
and stand behind the student-presentation block.

## Parked Arc II topics (restore by giving them taught weeks)
Kept in three places so they're easy to bring back: the **Week 1 deck** (a "Parked" slide + an HTML
comment), `shared/sem3-meta.js` (`window.SEM3_PARKED`), and here.

1. **Neg-Raising** — "I don't think he'll come" ⇝ "I think he won't." Excluded-middle presupposition
   vs strict-NPI-licensing. *Bartsch 1973; Gajewski 2007.*
2. **Embedded Questions & Responsive Predicates** — question denotations; exhaustivity
   (mention-some/all); predicates taking both complement types; the uniform-semantics program.
   *Karttunen 1977; Groenendijk & Stokhof 1984; Spector & Égré 2015; Theiler, Roelofsen & Aloni 2018; Uegaki 2019.*
3. **Selection & Anti-rogativity** — why "believe" hates "whether"; deriving selection rather than
   stipulating it. The active frontier; a natural home for an assessed squib.
   *Uegaki & Sudo 2019; Mayr 2019; Theiler, Roelofsen & Aloni 2019; Uegaki 2019.*
4. **Indexical Shift & Monsters** — Kaplan's prohibition and the data that breaks it (Amharic,
   Zazaki, Uyghur); shift-together; the loop back to de se.
   *Schlenker 2003; Anand & Nevins 2004; Deal 2020.*

## Week-by-week set readings (taught portion)

- **Wk 1 · Sense, Reference and Opacity.** Frege (1892), *On Sense and Reference*; Russell (1905),
  *On Denoting* (sel.); Carnap (1947), *Meaning and Necessity* ch. I.
- **Wk 2 · Frames and Accessibility.** Kripke (1963), *Semantical Considerations on Modal Logic*;
  Gamut / von Fintel & Heim notes.
- **Wk 3 · Quantifying over Alternatives.** Hintikka (1969), *Semantics for Propositional Attitudes*;
  Kratzer (1981/1991), conversational backgrounds; von Fintel & Heim notes.
- **Wk 4 · De Re, De Dicto, De Se.** Lewis (1979), *Attitudes De Dicto and De Se*; Percus & Sauerland
  (2003), *On the LFs of Attitude Reports*.
- **Wk 5 · The Granularity Problem.** Cresswell & von Stechow (1982), structured meanings; Moltmann
  (2017), cognitive products; Berto & Jago, *Impossible Worlds* (SEP) for orientation.
- **Wk 6 · Decomposing Attitude Verbs.** Kratzer (2006); Moulton (2015), *CPs*; Elliott (2017),
  *Elements of Clausal Embedding*; Davidson (1968), *On Saying That* (contrast).
- **Wk 8 · Factivity.** Kiparsky & Kiparsky (1970), *Fact*; Karttunen (1971), *Some Observations on
  Factivity*.
