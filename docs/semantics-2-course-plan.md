## The spine

Sem I built the **extensional** ontology: sets → predicates → quantifiers → events →
times. Sem II is **the intensional clausal spine**: it adds **possible worlds** and rebuilds
the operators that live above the verb — negation, modality, attitudes, conditionals,
aspect, evidentiality — at an advanced, cross-linguistic, open-problems level.

**Week 1 is a recap-and-pivot:** it reviews the Sem I formal system (model, ⟦·⟧, predicate
logic, λ, events) and then points past it — flagging the substitution failures and modal/
attitude contexts that motivate **possible worlds and intensions**. The machinery is then
built up front through **modality (Weeks 3–4)**: classical modal logic first → Kratzer's
modal-base + ordering-source system second (per your call). Attitudes reuse it rather than
motivate it.

**Order:** recap → negation → worlds/modal logic → Kratzer → attitudes & embedding →
[reading week] → conditionals → aspect → evidentiality → **Modals & Tense** capstone.
Modality precedes conditionals and evidentiality throughout.

## Per-week, through the four lenses

### Wk 1 — Recap & the Intensional Turn
- **Empirical** — where extensional meaning runs out: substitution failures (Hesperus/Phosphorus), modal & attitude contexts, non-referential readings — the data a truth-in-a-model theory can't capture.
- **Historical** — Frege's puzzle (morning/evening star); Carnap's intension/extension; Montague's step from an extensional to an intensional fragment.
- **Formal** — recap of the Sem I toolkit (domain, ⟦·⟧, predicate logic, λ, events, times); then the core new idea — an intension as a function from possible worlds (and times) to extensions, ⟦·⟧^{w,g}.
- **Typological** — is the inventory of semantic types universal? whether every language encodes the same intensional distinctions — the cross-linguistic thread for the term.

### Wk 2 — Negation
- **Empirical** — neg/quantifier scope ("everyone didn't leave"), NPI licensing (*any, ever, lift a finger*), neg-raising, metalinguistic negation, split scope, negative concord.
- **Historical** — Klima's negation tests (1964); Ladusaw on downward entailment & NPIs (1979); Horn, *A Natural History of Negation* (1989).
- **Formal** — ¬ as propositional operator; downward-entailing environments; scope at LF; Strawson entailment as NPI licenser.
- **Typological** — negative concord (Romance/Slavic) vs double-negation languages; Jespersen's Cycle; Miestamo's negation typology.

### Wk 3 — Worlds, Intensions & Modal Logic *(worlds machinery built)*
- **Empirical** — must/may/can/should; epistemic vs deontic vs circumstantial/ability; one form, many flavours; intensional contexts where extensions don't suffice.
- **Historical** — C.I. Lewis; Kripke (1963, accessibility semantics); von Wright (deontic logic).
- **Formal** — ◻/◇; frames & accessibility; the systems K/T/S4/S5; quantification over accessible worlds.
- **Typological** — modal concord; variable-force modals previewed; van der Auwera & Plungian's semantic map.

### Wk 4 — The Kratzer System
- **Empirical** — graded modality (must vs should vs might); "in view of…" phrases; weak necessity; comparative possibility.
- **Historical** — Kratzer (1977; 1981; collected in *Modals and Conditionals*, 2012).
- **Formal** — conversational background; modal base + ordering source; doubly-relative modality; flavours from context, not the lexicon.
- **Typological** — St'át'imcets variable-force modals (Rullmann, Matthewson & Davis 2008); Gitksan.

### Wk 5 — Attitude Reports
- **Empirical** — believe/know/want complements; de re vs de dicto; substitution failure; logical omniscience.
- **Historical** — Frege (Sinn/Bedeutung, oblique reference); Hintikka (1969); Quine, "Quantifiers and propositional attitudes" (1956).
- **Formal** — attitude verb = ∀ over accessible worlds; doxastic/epistemic accessibility (reusing Wk 3's intensions).
- **Typological** — complementiser systems; logophoricity; indexical shift (Amharic — Schlenker).

### Wk 6 — Factivity, Selection & Neg-Raising
- **Empirical** — factive presupposition (know/regret vs believe); responsive predicates (know whether); that/whether/Q selection; the neg-raising class.
- **Historical** — Kiparsky & Kiparsky, "Fact" (1970); Karttunen on implicative & factive verbs (1971); Gajewski on neg-raising as excluded middle.
- **Formal** — presupposition projection from complements; soft/hard triggers; excluded-middle presupposition deriving neg-raising.
- **Typological** — factivity marking; finite vs nominalised complementation (Noonan).

### Wk 8 — Conditionals I: Indicatives & the Restrictor Analysis
- **Empirical** — paradoxes of material implication; if + modal/Q-adverb; bare conditionals; the proportion problem.
- **Historical** — Lewis, "Adverbs of Quantification" (1975); Kratzer, "Conditionals" (1986); Stalnaker; the Ramsey test.
- **Formal** — if-clause restricts an operator; "no two-place *if* connective"; operator–restrictor–nuclear-scope.
- **Typological** — conditional markers; correlatives (Hindi); insubordinated conditionals.

### Wk 9 — Conditionals II: Counterfactuals & Similarity
- **Empirical** — would/might counterfactuals; Sobel & reverse-Sobel sequences; antecedent-strengthening failures.
- **Historical** — Stalnaker (1968); Lewis, *Counterfactuals* (1973); von Fintel (2001); Iatridou (2000).
- **Formal** — selection function / similarity ordering; the variably-strict conditional; non-monotonicity.
- **Typological** — counterfactual "fake past" / X-marking (Iatridou 2000); subjunctive vs indicative morphology.

### Wk 10 — Aspect & the Perfect
- **Empirical** — perfective/imperfective; the imperfective paradox; the perfect's readings; Aktionsart interactions.
- **Historical** — Vendler (1957); Dowty (1979); Klein (1994, topic time); Portner on the perfect.
- **Formal** — viewpoint aspect as topic-time/event-time relation (AspP); inertia worlds for the imperfective; perfect time span.
- **Typological** — Slavic aspect; Romance imparfait/passé; perfect vs perfective (Dahl, Bybee).

### Wk 11 — Evidentiality
- **Empirical** — direct/indirect/reportative/inferential; evidentials vs epistemic modals; at-issue vs not-at-issue; assent/dissent; the reportative exception.
- **Historical** — Aikhenvald (2004, typology); Faller (2002, Cuzco Quechua, illocutionary); Matthewson, Davis & Rullmann (2007, St'át'imcets, modal); Murray (2017).
- **Formal** — evidential-as-epistemic-modal vs illocutionary/speech-act operator; not-at-issue content & projection.
- **Typological** — Tariana, Tuyuca (5-way), Cuzco Quechua, St'át'imcets, Tibetan, Bulgarian/Turkish indirectivity.

### Wk 12 — Modals & Tense *(capstone)*
- **Empirical** — temporal orientation of modals (epistemic vs root); actuality entailments ("Jean a pu soulever la table — et il l'a fait"); modal + aspect interactions.
- **Historical** — Condoravdi (2002); Bhatt (1999, ability & actuality entailments); Hacquard (2006/2010); Stowell on modal tense.
- **Formal** — modal temporal perspective vs orientation; aspect scoping over modality → actuality entailment.
- **Typological** — actuality entailments across French, Hindi, Greek; future-as-modal systems.

## Formal escalation (the "Formal" lens, as a curriculum)

1. Recap of the extensional toolkit; intension = function from worlds *(wk 1)*
2. Negation/polarity: downward entailment, LF scope, Strawson entailment *(wk 2)*
3. Modal logic: ◻/◇, frames, K/T/S4/S5 *(wk 3)*
4. Kratzer: modal base + ordering source, doubly-relative modality *(wk 4)*
5. Attitudes: ∀ over doxastic/epistemic accessibility; presupposition projection *(wk 5–6)*
6. Restrictor analysis & tripartite quantification *(wk 8)*
7. Similarity ordering / selection functions / variably-strict conditional *(wk 9)*
8. Aspect: topic time, AspP, inertia worlds *(wk 10)*; not-at-issue content *(wk 11)*
9. Temporal perspective/orientation + aspect-over-modality *(wk 12)*

## Readings spine

Taught from a combination of three texts, with primary papers per week:

- **Coppock & Champollion, *Invitation to Formal Semantics*** — the modern intro backbone; carries the intensions/worlds/modality build.

- **Portner, *Modality* (OUP, 2009)** — depth for the modality and conditionals block.
- **Primary papers** per week (anchor column): Kripke 1963, Kratzer 1986/1991, Lewis 1973, Iatridou 2000, Klein 1994, Faller 2002, Condoravdi 2002, Hacquard 2010.
