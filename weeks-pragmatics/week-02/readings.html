Week 2 · Readings

Speech Acts

Reading list Figures: Austin · Searle

Three sources for Week 2 — a textbook chapter introducing speech-act theory, and two classical sources: Austin's founding lectures, and Searle's taxonomy of illocutionary acts. References are in APA; open-access links are given where available. Read the textbook chapter first, then Austin. Confirm editions and availability against the library / Blackboard list before circulating.

Reading list one textbook · two classical
  1. TextbookGrundy, P. (2008). Speech acts: Language as action. In Doing pragmatics (3rd ed.). Hodder Education.
    Performatives, felicity, and indirect speech acts — with live data and exercises to work through.
    Purchase
  2. ClassicalAustin, J. L. (1962). How to do things with words (Lectures I & VIII–X). Clarendon Press.
    From performatives to the locutionary / illocutionary / perlocutionary distinction — the founding text.
    Purchase
  3. ClassicalSearle, J. R. (1976). A classification of illocutionary acts. Language in Society, 5(1), 1–23. https://doi.org/10.1017/S0047404500006837
    The five-way taxonomy — assertives, directives, commissives, expressives, declarations.
    Library login JSTOR
