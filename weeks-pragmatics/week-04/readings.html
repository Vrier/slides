Week 4 · Readings

Presupposition

Reading list Figures: Strawson · Russell

Three sources for Week 4 — a textbook chapter on presupposition, the classical paper that made presupposition a pragmatic notion, and a contemporary theory of how presuppositions project. References are in APA; open-access links are given where available. Read the textbook chapter first, then Strawson. Confirm editions and availability against the library / Blackboard list before circulating.

Reading list one textbook · one classical · one contemporary
  1. TextbookGrundy, P. (2008). Presupposition. In Doing pragmatics (3rd ed.). Hodder Education.
    Triggers, projection, and the semantic / pragmatic split — with data and exercises to work through.
    Purchase
  2. ClassicalStrawson, P. F. (1950). On referring. Mind, 59(235), 320–344. https://doi.org/10.1093/mind/LIX.235.320
    Presupposition as the alternative to Russell — reference and the failure of the King of France.
    Library login JSTOR
  3. ContemporarySchlenker, P. (2008). Be articulate: A pragmatic theory of presupposition projection. Theoretical Linguistics, 34(3), 157–212. https://doi.org/10.1515/THLI.2008.013
    A leading contemporary account of the projection problem — presupposition as a by-product of being maximally explicit.
    Library login DOI
